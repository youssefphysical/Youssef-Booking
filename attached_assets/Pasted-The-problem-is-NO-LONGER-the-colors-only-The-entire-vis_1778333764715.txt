The problem is NO LONGER the colors only.

The entire visual composition still feels like:
a modern SaaS/mobile dashboard.

That is the core issue.

Even after the palette changes:
the homepage structure still reads as:
“fitness startup UI”.

We must now change the VISUAL LANGUAGE itself.

STOP designing the homepage as:

- stacked SaaS cards
- boxed mobile widgets
- dashboard sections
- repeated rounded rectangles
- evenly spaced app blocks

The current layout is:
too flat,
too vertical,
too component-based,
too generic.

NEW DESIGN DIRECTION:

The homepage must feel like:
a cinematic Tron Legacy interface experience.

This means:

1. LESS BOXES
   Reduce visible card containers significantly.

Many sections should blend directly into the AMOLED black background instead of looking like isolated cards.

2. MORE CINEMATIC COMPOSITION
   Use:

- layered depth
- glow separation
- asymmetric spacing
- atmospheric transitions
- oversized typography
- cinematic negative space
- floating content composition

3. BLACK VOID DOMINANCE
   Large portions of the screen should intentionally remain dark.

Do NOT fill every area with blue surfaces or bordered containers.

4. HERO SECTION REBUILD
   The hero should feel:

- immersive
- dramatic
- cinematic
- contrast-heavy

NOT:
like a normal landing page header.

The image should blend into darkness naturally.
Allow the black background to consume edges of the composition.

5. REMOVE “APP UI” FEELING
   Right now the homepage feels:

- too informational
- too dashboard-like
- too segmented

Reduce:

- repetitive cards
- repeated outlines
- repeated pills
- excessive borders

6. TRON ENERGY LINES
   Introduce subtle cinematic energy-line design language inspired by Tron Legacy:

- thin glowing separators
- ambient horizon lines
- directional light streaks
- subtle edge glows
- energy pathways

Very subtle.
Very premium.
Never gaming UI.

7. TYPOGRAPHY
   Current typography hierarchy is too small and safe.

Make sections feel:
editorial,
cinematic,
luxury,
premium.

Use:

- larger headings
- stronger contrast
- cleaner spacing
- less clutter
- more breathing room

8. MOBILE EXPERIENCE
   Mobile should feel like:
   a futuristic cinematic interface,
   NOT:
   a stacked card app.

The user should emotionally feel:

- immersion
- atmosphere
- premium depth
- futuristic luxury

9. IMPORTANT:
   Keep:

- readability
- smooth scrolling
- performance
- accessibility

But visually:
move AWAY from SaaS.
Move INTO cinematic sci-fi luxury.

10. FINAL TARGET EMOTION:
    When someone opens the homepage,
    it should feel like:
    “a premium futuristic experience”

NOT:
“a fitness booking template”.

The dashboard is now visually much stronger.

Next priority is:
human guidance.

Right now the UI is becoming premium visually,
but some areas still feel like a system.

We need it to feel like:
a real coach guiding the client.

Final human-centered UX pass:

1. Every section should answer:
   “Why does this matter to me?”

Do not show data without emotional context.

Examples:

Instead of:
“Water Target”

Use:
“Hydration Goal”

+ helper:
  “Better hydration improves recovery and performance.”

Instead of:
“Streak”

Use:
“Consistency Streak”

+ helper:
  “Small consistent actions create long-term results.”

2. Add subtle onboarding guidance
   Many first-time clients won’t understand:

- InBody
- supplements
- streaks
- recovery
- check-ins
- progress photos

Add:
small info icons / helper sheets / tap-to-explain micro-guidance.

But:
Keep them lightweight.
Never create clutter.

3. Progressive disclosure
   Do NOT show everything at once.

New clients should see:

- next action
- first booking
- upload scan
- choose goal

Advanced sections should appear later naturally.

The dashboard should “grow” with the client journey.

4. Reduce cognitive load
   Important:

A client opening this app after work or training should feel:
clarity,
motivation,
simplicity.

Avoid:

- too many equal cards
- too many labels
- too many competing actions

One screen = one dominant purpose.

5. Coaching tone
   Replace technical/admin wording with coaching language.

Avoid:

- “No active supplement”
- “No records”
- “Pending”
- “Incomplete”

Prefer:

- “Your supplement plan will appear here.”
- “Start tracking your progress.”
- “Ready for your first scan.”
- “Your journey begins here.”

6. Emotional momentum
   The app should constantly create:
   small wins.

Examples:

- progress chips
- encouraging milestones
- completion feedback
- tiny celebrations
- gentle motivation

Not exaggerated gamification.
Just momentum.

7. Accessibility & readability
   Very important on mobile:

- maintain strong contrast
- avoid tiny helper text
- avoid thin gray text on dark backgrounds
- ensure all touch targets feel effortless

8. Final UX target
   The experience should feel like:

“a luxury private coaching system that simplifies fitness.”

NOT:
a management dashboard.

The client should always feel:
guided,
supported,
clear,
motivated,
never overwhelmed.
